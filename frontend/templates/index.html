Pipeline

Neural Workflow

01

Input Biometrics

Submit eight key clinical metrics including Glucose, BMI, Age, Blood Pressure, Insulin, Skin Thickness, Pregnancies, and Pedigree Function for comprehensive analysis.

02

Neural Processing

The logistic regression model applies trained weights via SGD optimization, standardizing inputs and computing sigmoid-activated probability scores.

03

Risk Report

Full diagnostic report with probability scoring, radar chart visualization, and printable export for clinical records.

Biometrics

Key Risk Factors

Glucose

Fasting blood sugar level. Elevated glucose is a primary diabetes indicator. Normal range: 70-100 mg/dL.

BMI

Body Mass Index calculated from height and weight. Higher BMI correlates strongly with insulin resistance.

Age

Advancing age increases diabetes risk. The model accounts for age-related metabolic changes.

Blood Pressure

Hypertension often co-occurs with diabetes. Diastolic pressure is used in the model.

Insulin

Serum insulin level. Abnormal insulin production signals pancreatic dysfunction.

Skin Thickness

Triceps skin fold thickness used as an indicator of body fat percentage.

Pregnancies

Number of pregnancies. History of gestational diabetes increases future risk.

Pedigree Function

Diabetes pedigree function scoring genetic predisposition based on family history.
